# Charlie Munger Mental Models

Apply Charlie Munger's lattice of mental models—a multidisciplinary thinking framework that combines concepts from economics, psychology, mathematics, physics, and biology to analyze problems from multiple perspectives.

## Core Principles

**1. Lattice of Mental Models (思維格柵)**
Combine multiple mental models from different disciplines to see problems from various angles. No single model provides complete understanding.

**2. Inversion (逆向思考)**
Think backwards: instead of "How do we succeed?", ask "How do we fail?" and avoid those paths. Often clearer to identify what to avoid than what to pursue.

**3. Circle of Competence (能力圈)**
Know what you know and what you don't know. Stay within your circle of competence or expand it systematically. The edge of the circle is where danger lies.

**4. Second-Order Thinking (二階思考)**
Consider not just immediate consequences but also the consequences of consequences. Ask "And then what?"

## Mental Models Quick Reference

For detailed explanations and application examples, see `references/mental-models.md`.

**Core models to consider:**
- Economics: Opportunity cost, incentives, supply/demand, economies of scale, network effects
- Psychology: Confirmation bias, social proof, commitment & consistency, scarcity, reciprocation
- Mathematics: Probability thinking, compounding, permutations, regression to mean
- Physics: Critical mass, leverage, momentum, friction
- Biology: Evolution, natural selection, adaptation, ecosystems

**Selection heuristic**: Choose models that reveal hidden dynamics or challenge conventional assumptions about the situation.
